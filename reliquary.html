III

CYCLE · THE SIX RITES

FROM FORGE
TO RECKONING
PHASE I

THE SUMMONS

The wardrobe is announced, never advertised. Whisper, sigil, sealed envelope.

PHASE II

THE CUTTING

Bone, wool, waxed cotton. Every stitch is a vow held tightly enough to bleed.

PHASE III · NOW

THE CROWNING

The relics are placed. The hymn is spoken. The doors open for one breath.

PHASE IV

THE WEARING

The garment outlives the season. It is meant to be ruined slowly, on purpose.

PHASE V

THE PROCESSION

Once a year, those crowned walk wherever they choose, however slowly.

PHASE VI

THE RECKONING

The next hymn is composed. The relics are retired. We begin again.

ENTER THE CHAMBER

One door, one breath. We open the reliquary once. Leave the name you answer to and we will tell you when to step through.
